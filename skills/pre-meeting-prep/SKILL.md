# Pre-Meeting Prep

Set the right internal state, confirm the single most important outcome, and prepare opening language — before entering the room.

> **v1 — Baseline.** Upgrade when Executive Buy-in Presentation System course PDF arrives (29 March 2026). Update after each real meeting with what worked and what didn't.

## The Purpose

The meeting starts before you enter the room. Internal state, intention, and mental preparation determine whether Lorian responds from a grounded, curious place — or from the defaults (direct push-back, silence, internal frustration) that undermine the outcome.

This skill covers the 15-30 minutes before the meeting — and the 2-3 minute social window as the room assembles.

## Workflow

### Phase 1 — Internal State Check

1. **Assess internal state** — Ask Lorian directly:
   - "On a scale of 1-10, how grounded and curious are you right now — or are you defensive, anxious, or already rehearsing arguments?"
   - "What is the emotional charge around this meeting? Is there anyone in that room you're already in conflict with?"

2. **Diagnose the state:**
   - **Grounded and curious** → proceed to Phase 2
   - **Mildly tense** → brief reset before Phase 2 (see reset techniques below)
   - **Defensive or anxious** → full reset required before anything else; do not skip this

3. **Reset techniques (match to what Lorian finds most effective):**
   - **Breathing reset:** 4 counts in, hold 4, out 6. Repeat 3 times. Activates the parasympathetic nervous system; physiologically reduces the threat response.
   - **Intention reframe:** "I am not going in to win an argument or be proved right. I am going in to help this team make the best decision for the business. Their resistance is information, not an attack."
   - **Visualisation:** See the meeting ending well — specific images of the decision being made, handshakes, the exec saying yes. The brain does not fully distinguish visualised success from real success; it primes approach mode.
   - **Identity shift:** "In this room, I am an ally, not an analyst. I am a thinking partner, not a presenter."
   - **The single focus:** "If only one thing goes well in this meeting, what is it?" Hold that single outcome. Everything else is secondary.

### Phase 2 — Persona and Objection Review

4. **Review the exec personas** — For each person in the room:
   - What do they care about most?
   - What is their relationship to this recommendation?
   - What is their most likely objection type?
   - What single thing to watch for in the first five minutes?

5. **Identify the highest-resistance person** — Set a specific intention toward them:
   - Not "I will overcome their resistance"
   - But: "I want to understand their concern. Their resistance is protecting something that matters to them. My job is to find out what."

6. **Review the objection map from WF3/WF4** — For each anticipated objection:
   - What is the prepared response?
   - What is the concession in reserve (if needed)?
   - What is the Third Option move if push-back or silence is triggered?

7. **Confirm the single most important outcome** — One sentence:
   - "If I leave this room having achieved only one thing, it is [specific decision / commitment / agreement]."
   - Hold this throughout. Everything else is secondary.

### Phase 3 — Opening Preparation

8. **Confirm the opening sentence** — From WF4:
   - Say it out loud, not in your head
   - Does it feel natural and confident, or scripted and nervous?
   - If scripted: practise the intention behind it, not the words

9. **Prepare the psychological safety frame** — The opening must establish the meeting as a shared problem, not a verdict:
   - "I want to think through this together — your input will make the recommendation better, not just validate it."
   - Or, more directly: "I've got a view, and I want to test it against the room."
   - Avoid: opening with authority ("I've done the analysis and the conclusion is...") — it closes down input before it starts

10. **Plan the social window** — The 2-3 minutes as the room assembles:
    - Genuine question for the most resistant exec: about something non-work, or a genuine follow-up to a previous conversation
    - The goal: arrive at the formal opening with the relationship already slightly warmer than it was
    - One human moment before the business starts — not forced, not performed

### Phase 4 — Final Check

11. **Final pre-entry check:**
    - Am I clear on the single most important outcome?
    - Am I going in to help, or to be right?
    - Do I have the first sentence ready?
    - Is there a reset technique available if internal state shifts in the room?

## Outputs

Delivered in the conversation before the meeting.

- Internal state assessment and reset (if needed)
- Persona quick-reference: one key watch-out per exec
- Single most important outcome — stated clearly
- Opening sentence (confirmed or refined)
- Psychological safety framing for the opening
- Social window plan (one genuine human moment)
- Intention statement for the highest-resistance person

## Guidelines

- Internal state check is non-negotiable — if Lorian is already in defensive mode, no amount of content preparation will save the meeting
- The reset techniques are not optional extras — they are evidence-based performance tools; treat them as such
- The single most important outcome must be specific enough to know whether it was achieved at the end of the meeting
- After delivering, ask: "Say the opening sentence out loud. Does it feel like you, or does it feel like a presentation?"
- Proactively flag: "The most common pre-meeting failure is over-preparing the content and under-preparing the state. You know the content. The question is whether you're in the right place to deliver it."
- Humor and warmth start here — the social window is where the relationship temperature is set before the formal meeting begins
